Coins and Bills

Both coins and bills are forms of money that people use to buy things they need or want. Coins and bills are part of the broader currency system that facilitates economic transactions.

Coins represent different values, such as one cent, five cents, ten cents, and so on. They come in different sizes, colors, and shapes, with each denomination having a unique design.

Bills are flat pieces of paper issued by the government. They come in different denominations, each representing a specific value.
